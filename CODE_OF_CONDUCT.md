# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Tetromino project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful** — Treat others with dignity and respect. Disagreement is natural, but approach it constructively.
- **Assume good intentions** — Most people are trying to help. Give others the benefit of the doubt.
- **Be constructive in feedback** — Offer specific, actionable suggestions rather than vague criticism. Celebrate wins and acknowledge effort.
- **Be inclusive** — Welcome newcomers, help others learn, and make space for different perspectives and experience levels.
- **Be patient** — Remember that English may not be everyone's first language, and that text-based communication can obscure tone.
- **Stay on topic** — Keep discussions relevant to Tetromino, Are.na, Obsidian, and the task at hand.

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable within our community:

- **Harassment** — Deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, or unwanted attention.
- **Discrimination** — Biased or prejudiced treatment based on personal characteristics.
- **Trolling and inflammatory comments** — Posting inflammatory or off-topic messages to provoke others.
- **Spam** — Repeated irrelevant or promotional content, including unsolicited advertisements.
- **Threats** — Threats of violence or harm toward any individual or group.
- **Doxxing** — Publishing others' private information without permission.
- **Sexual harassment** — Unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind.

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it to the maintainers as soon as possible.

**How to report:**

- **Preferred:** Open a private discussion on GitHub (if available) or contact the maintainers via the project's GitHub profile.
- **Alternative:** Email the maintainers directly. If you do not have a direct email, reach out via GitHub and request a private communication channel.

Please include:
- Your contact information (so we can follow up).
- Names (real or usernames) of any individuals involved.
- Description of the behavior, with dates/times and context if possible.
- Any additional information that may be helpful.

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. You will not face retaliation for reporting in good faith.

## Response Process

When a report is received, maintainers will:

1. **Acknowledge** receipt of the report within 48 hours.
2. **Investigate** by reviewing evidence and, if necessary, speaking with involved parties.
3. **Decide** on appropriate action based on the severity and context of the violation.
4. **Communicate** the outcome to the reporter (and the violator, where appropriate), respecting confidentiality.
5. **Follow up** to ensure the behavior has stopped and the community remains healthy.

## Enforcement

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to enforce this Code of Conduct. Consequences for unacceptable behavior may include:

- **Warning** — A private, written warning with an explanation of what was inappropriate and expectations going forward.
- **Temporary ban** — A temporary suspension from interactions in the community (issues, discussions, PRs) for a specified period.
- **Permanent ban** — Permanent exclusion from the community for repeated or severe violations.

Enforcement decisions are made at the discretion of the maintainers with the goal of preserving a safe and productive community for everyone.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other official project channels — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces (e.g., conference talks, blog posts, social media).
